NYtimes

Visitor Analysis

Target Audience:

What are their demographics: NYtimes is visited by 60% men, and 40% women. The average age that visits the site the most is between 38-58. People that visit the site make an average income of more than $80.000 per year. The average education that people have who visit this site is 60% bachelor degree holders, 28% is graduates, and the rest is highschool or no schooling.

How did the site meet the needs of the visitor? The site did really well to show what they stand for, a news agency that delivers trusted news and information.

Describe how the content was organized to facilitate it being found: The content was simple and nice looking. In the home page was not much information, but people still can find easily the news category they want to by a few clicks (which is made really easy through the navigation bar)

What did the site do to make the mobile experience enjoyable? I like that its very simple, the colors have a nice intensity to it, easy to navigate and the fonts and design is beautiful. Also, the loading time is fast also.

Did the site seem to facilitate people in a mobile environment “Visitor Considerations”? It does seem to facilitate so mobile users can easily use it, and find what they need. I definetely will be using it again.

Persona

People who us NYtimes are well educated and looking for a reliable news source. The site offers the followings and a few other things like breaking news, multimedia, reviews & opinion on Washington, business, sports, movies, travel, books, jobs, education, real estate and so on.

Persona Groups:

Businessperson

In this group are people who are interested in doing business or do business at the moment. The New York Times has really good information in this field, such as the stock market, latest breaking corporate news and so on.

Politics

In this group are people interested in learning more about political news, events, scandals and so on. From the rumors that might change a campaign to the latest Presidential, local voting results, to what happens all around the world with people that are in power.

Opinions

In this group are people that are looking for opinions in important fields such as economics, politics, war, conflicts and Observations and provocations from opinion writers in Washington and around the world.

Site Contrast:

  1. The site I liked the least was Gigaom
  2. They do a good job providing realted news to what their visitors are looking for.
  3. Their visitors get most of the information they want, but it takes a long time, thus not reaching the max they could come up with.
  4. The website is orginasied well for the most part
  5. Mobile users dont have as good experience that desktop users do. Given the fact that most of the online users come from a mobile, the site needs to change to accomodate that. Navigation is a bit harder, especially if you look for something specific. On a deskpot its easier to navigate.
